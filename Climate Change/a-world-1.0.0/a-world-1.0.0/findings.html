1. Kazakistan

Kazakhstan, officially the Republic of Kazakhstan, is a landlocked country mostly in Central Asia, with a part in Eastern Europe.

Average monthly temperature and rainfall in Kazakhstan (1991–2020).

The graph depicts the average monthly temperature and rainfall in Kazakhstan from 1991 to 2020. The temperature, represented by the black line, shows a clear seasonal pattern with temperatures rising from January, peaking in July, and then gradually decreasing until December. The highest average temperatures occur in July, reaching approximately 25°C, while the lowest temperatures are in January, dropping to around -10°C. Rainfall, shown by the blue bars, also exhibits a seasonal trend. Rainfall increases from January, reaching its peak in June and July, with each of these months receiving around 25 mm of rainfall. After July, the rainfall gradually decreases, with the lowest levels observed in December and January, around 5 mm. This graph illustrates the climatic pattern in Kazakhstan, characterized by warm, wet summers and cold, dry winters.


2. Pakistan

Pakistan, officially the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, is a country located in South Asia. It is bordered by India to the east, China to the northeast. It has a coastline along the Arabian Sea to the south.

Historical trend of climate change in South Pakistan

The graph presents the historical trend of climate change in South Pakistan from 2000 to 2018, showcasing four key metrics: minimum annual temperature, maximum annual temperature, average annual temperature, and annual precipitation. The blue line, representing the minimum annual temperature, shows considerable fluctuations, indicating variability in the lowest temperatures each year. The red line for the maximum annual temperature remains relatively stable, suggesting minor variations in the highest temperatures. The green line for the average annual temperature also demonstrates some fluctuations but remains relatively consistent. Lastly, the purple line representing annual precipitation shows significant variability, highlighting changes in rainfall patterns over the years. Overall, the graph provides a comprehensive view of the climatic changes in South Pakistan, emphasizing temperature and precipitation trends.
