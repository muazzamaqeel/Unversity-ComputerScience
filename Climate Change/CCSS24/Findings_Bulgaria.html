COUNTRY OVERVIEW

The Republic of Bulgaria is located in Southeast Europe, bordered by Romania to the north, Serbia and North Macedonia to the west, Greece and Turkey to the south, and the Black Sea to the east. Bulgaria's landscape is diverse, featuring mountains, plains, and a coastline. The country has a population of approximately 6.5 million (2024), with a significant portion living in urban areas such as the capital city, Sofia.

Bulgaria's economy is classified as an upper-middle-income economy. The service sector is the largest contributor to the GDP, accounting for around 64%, followed by industry at 30%, and agriculture at 6%. Major industries include energy, mining, metallurgy, machinery, textiles, and tourism. Bulgaria has seen steady economic growth with a GDP per capita of $12,650 in 2023, up from $12,100 in 2022.

Climate Baseline

Bulgaria has a diverse climate, influenced by its varied geography and proximity to the Mediterranean and Black Seas. The climate can be broadly categorized into continental, Mediterranean, and alpine. The northern regions experience a continental climate with hot summers and cold winters, while the southern regions have a more Mediterranean climate with milder winters and hot summers. The mountainous areas have an alpine climate with colder temperatures and significant snowfall in winter.

Average annual temperatures in Bulgaria range from 10°C to 13°C, with January being the coldest month (average temperatures between -2°C and 2°C) and July being the warmest month (average temperatures between 20°C and 25°C). Precipitation varies across the country, with the northern and western regions receiving more rainfall compared to the southern and coastal regions. The average annual precipitation is around 650 mm, with the mountains receiving up to 1,000 mm and the coastal areas receiving around 400 mm.

Key Impacts of Climate Change in Bulgaria

Temperature Rise

Bulgaria has experienced a noticeable increase in average temperatures over the past few decades, consistent with global warming trends. Between 1961 and 2023, the average temperature increased by approximately 0.5°C per decade. This warming is more pronounced in the summer months, leading to more frequent and intense heatwaves. These changes affect agriculture, water resources, and human health, necessitating adaptation measures to mitigate the impacts.

Water Resources

Climate change impacts Bulgaria's water resources by altering precipitation patterns and increasing the frequency of extreme weather events. Reduced snow cover in the mountains and changing rainfall patterns lead to variability in river flows and water availability. This affects agriculture, hydropower generation, and water supply for households and industries. Effective water management strategies are needed to address these challenges and ensure sustainable water use.

Agriculture

Bulgaria's agricultural sector is highly vulnerable to climate change due to its reliance on favorable weather conditions. Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns, along with an increase in extreme weather events, pose risks to crop yields and livestock. Key crops such as wheat, corn, and sunflower are affected by droughts and heatwaves. Adaptation strategies include developing drought-resistant crop varieties, improving irrigation practices, and adopting climate-smart agriculture techniques.

Forestry

Bulgaria's forests, which cover about one-third of the country, are also impacted by climate change. Increased temperatures and changes in precipitation patterns have heightened the risk of forest fires, pest infestations, and diseases. These factors contribute to forest degradation and loss of biodiversity. Sustainable forest management practices and reforestation with climate-resilient species are essential to protect and preserve Bulgaria's forests.

Biodiversity

Climate change threatens Bulgaria's rich biodiversity, impacting various ecosystems and species. Shifts in temperature and precipitation patterns alter habitats and the distribution of species. Those unable to adapt face increased risks of extinction. Conservation efforts, including the establishment of protected areas and ecological networks, are crucial to preserving biodiversity and enhancing the resilience of ecosystems.

Mitigation and Adaptation Efforts

Renewable Energy

Bulgaria is increasing its investment in renewable energy sources to reduce its reliance on fossil fuels and lower greenhouse gas emissions. The country is focusing on expanding wind, solar, and biomass energy production. By 2023, renewable energy accounted for around 20% of Bulgaria's total energy consumption, with plans to increase this share through supportive policies and technological advancements.

Climate Policy

Bulgaria has implemented national strategies and action plans to address climate change and align with the European Union's climate goals. The National Climate Change Adaptation Strategy outlines measures to enhance resilience across sectors, including agriculture, water management, and infrastructure. These policies aim to reduce carbon emissions, promote energy efficiency, and support climate adaptation initiatives.

Sustainable Land Management

Sustainable land management practices are crucial for addressing the impacts of climate change on agriculture and forestry. Bulgaria promotes organic farming, soil conservation techniques, and sustainable forestry practices. Reforestation projects and the restoration of degraded lands are also being implemented to enhance carbon sequestration and improve land resilience.

Water Management

Effective water management is essential to cope with the challenges posed by climate change. Bulgaria invests in modernizing water infrastructure, improving irrigation efficiency, and developing integrated water resource management plans. These efforts aim to ensure a reliable water supply, mitigate flood risks, and enhance the resilience of water systems to climate variability and change.
